
Who are we: 
 Bruce House is committed to ensuring our clients have access to equality, housing stability, supported independence, and opportunities for healthy living.
 
 Our clientele includes women, men and children living with HIV who may be dealing with addiction, mental health issues, homelessness and incarceration. We support a diverse population including members of the LGBTQ community, ACB(African, Caribbean, and Black) communities and Indigenous communities as well as newcomers to Canada.
 
 Housing is a fundamental requirement for the health and well-being of people living with HIV. Bruce House clients benefit from increased housing stability, increased physical and emotional wellness, increased access to resources, decreased in isolation, and the peace of mind that comes from knowing a support network exists for them in times of need.
 
 Since opening its doors in 1988, through the generous support of local individuals and organizations, Bruce House has been delivering a "housing first" approach for people in our community living with HIV.
 
 Bruce House is the only organization in Ottawa with the core mission of providing housing and compassionate support to people living with HIV,
 
 Toady HIV is a manageable medical condition which comes with significant health and social impacts. Bruce House programs have evolved to meet these changing needs in an environment which respects the dignity and self-determination of the individual.
 
 Our programs and services have a positive impact on the day-to-day lives of individuals, families, and our neighborhoods. With the rising need for housing, and support for those now aging with HIV, Bruce House is committed to supporting clients so that there are no gaps resulting in housing instability, food insecurity and deteriorating health.

Programs and Services
 Bruce House provides housing and support services to people living with HIV based on the principle that secure housing is critically important in managing HIV. 
 Our programs and services have the following objectives:
 - Provide people living with HIV with access to secure, affordable and appropriate housing;
 - Provide services and supports to people living with HIV to help improve their health and life skills, increase their participation in the community, and foster social connection;
 - Support our clients with their specific needs to improve their quality of life
 - Ensure our programs and services reflect our values and are integrated to include prevention, education care, support and treatment.
 Outreach & Support (REST Program)
  The REST program (Support and Outreach) provides services and supports to people living with HIV, improving their health and life skills and increasing their participation in the community. This program provides support with their specific needs, short-term and long-term goals, and in the event of episodic crisis. We off wellness checks, transportation for groceries, finding housing, bus vouchers and food gift cards, resources in the community and accompaniment to the medical and social service appointments etc.. This program is highly individualized and meets clients where they are at. Clients have the opportunity to attend various monthly programs; these include Community Kitchen, Art Workshops, socials and information sessions.
 Supportive Independent Living Program (SLIP)
  The Supported Independent Living Program (Apartment Program) provides access to secure, safe, affordable and stable housing for families, individuals, and couples, in 45 subsidized apartments. Bruce House supports these residents in activities of daily living and provides access to other agencies and services in the community. Wellness checks, referrals, and programming such as the Community Kitchen and Buddy Program help to create social connections. This program is for people living with HIV who want to live independently with minimal supports.
 
  Clients have the opportunity to attend various monthly programs; these include Community Kitchen, Art Workshops, socials and information sessions.
 Volunteer Supports for Clients
  The Bruce House **Volunteer Program** includes a very important component that supports our clients who are marginalized or face social isolation: the **Buddy Program**.  Through this program a client is paired up with a volunteer who will provide support around psycho-social needs to reduce social isolation.

  
  Clients who wish to learn about the supports currently available to them from volunteers should speak with any Bruce House program staff.  You can can fill out the form at right to request a Buddy.
 Holistic Care
  Bruce House clients have the opportunity to attend a variety of health and wellness programs designed to decrease social isolation, improve life skills, and improve overall health and wellness.  These may include:
  - Community Kitchen: clients prepare a meal as a group, learning nutrition and cooking skills and having plenty of leftovers to take home.
  - Arts & Crafts Workshops: from making clocks with old records to making plant hangers and painting, these workshops offer fun craft projects—no previous experience is needed, and most projects can be completed in an afternoon.
  - Lunch Socials: clients gather for a free meal and companionship.
  - Games: from trivia to bingo, fun evenings for all.
  - Movies: grab the popcorn and gather ’round the TV.
  - Guest Speakers: learn about a variety of topics ranging from health and wellness to legal concerns and life skills.
  - Pantry: helps clients facing occasional shortages of food and toiletries, as well as provide supplies for people setting up new homes.
  - Complementary Therapies: foot care, massage, reiki, therapeutic touch (when available).

Becoming a Client
 If you are living with HIV and need housing or support, we are here to help. 

 We can help you sign up with the city’s Housing Registry and also add you to our own wait list for an apartment.  We can assist with food and other basic needs, as well as offer support and referrals.

 The first step is to be registered as a Bruce House client.  This will require a meeting with our program staff and answering some intake questions to help us understand your needs, and getting your consent to be added to our database.
